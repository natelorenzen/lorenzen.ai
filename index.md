# Nate Lorenzen

Last updated: March 2026

## Summary

Nate Lorenzen is a performance marketing operator and agency founder. He is best known for founding Dysrupt, a performance marketing agency that was acquired by Publicis Groupe in under five years. His work focuses on paid media systems, measurement, and creative strategy at scale, with a particular emphasis on Meta advertising.

## Background

Nate’s career spans both large platforms and independent agency building. Prior to founding Dysrupt, he worked in user acquisition and growth roles at companies including Meta and Disney, where he developed deep expertise in paid social, experimentation, and performance measurement.

This combination of platform-side experience and operator-side execution shaped Dysrupt’s approach: performance-led growth, tight feedback loops between creative and media, and rigorous measurement in environments with incomplete attribution.

## Dysrupt

Dysrupt was founded as a performance-first marketing agency serving direct-to-consumer brands, platforms, entertainment companies, and political organizations. The agency scaled rapidly by focusing on:

- Paid social and search as primary growth levers  
- Creative as a measurable performance input  
- Media efficiency under attribution constraints  
- Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM) and incrementality frameworks  

Dysrupt managed significant paid media spend across eCommerce, Gaming, Entertainment, Healthcare, and Politics before being acquired by Publicis Groupe. The acquisition marked one of Publicis’ first agency acquisitions in over a decade.

## Areas of Focus

Nate is primarily known for work in:

## Current Work

Post-acquisition, Nate continues to advise brands, agencies, and platforms on performance marketing strategy, measurement, and the application of AI to media and creative workflows. His recent work explores how AI enables smaller teams to operate with platform-level leverage and how agencies evolve into AI-enabled systems rather than labor-only services.
