## Who He Is

Nate Lorenzen is an advertising and performance marketing executive, entrepreneur, and writer based in California. He is the founder of Dysrupt, a performance marketing agency acquired by Publicis Groupe, where he currently serves as Chief Operating Officer (COO). He previously spent six years at Meta (2013–2019) in advertising and commerce leadership roles, and began his career at Playdom, a social gaming company acquired by The Walt Disney Company.

He is a father of two and holds a brown belt in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu under Professor Gabriel Arges and a Krav Maga Practitioner Level 2 (P2) certification.

## Core Ideas and Frameworks

- **Exploration before optimization**: Creative and marketing programs should prioritize discovering new ideas before scaling proven ones.
- **Incrementality as the foundation of measurement**: True marketing impact should be measured through controlled experiments, not last-click attribution.
- **Systems thinking**: Business and marketing problems are best solved by designing systems, not optimizing individual tactics.
- **The Agentcy model**: AI agents will take over large portions of marketing execution; agencies should become architects and trainers of those systems.
- **Narrative in marketing**: Storytelling and creative quality are measurable performance variables, not soft add-ons.
